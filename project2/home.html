Many children in Africa are desperate to go to school and learn how to read and write. But most of the students cannot because they lack funding. With parents already burdened with school fees, parents have to reprioritize the family needs.

To bring this matter closer home, most kenyan children have to walk long distances to get to school and usually very early in the morning. Our children trek long distances for a long period of time and by the time they arrive at school, they are exhausted even before the day starts for learning. It is also crucial to understand that they make the same way back home and will get home when it's late. It becomes a safety concern of when and where a child is away from a parent's sight. From the early morning commute, to the late evening commute, alot can happen especially with the rise of sexual predators, human trafficking and children getting lost on their way back home.

Most parents are also skeptical that the funds they give their children for bus fares will be lost during play or the students decide to use the funds alternatively for snacks or toys.

Tap to school utilizes a fintech technology (NFC/RFID) and gps locator where children are able to wear wristbands that are equipped with funds that can be used as virtual fares for their commute to school. Parents are able to recharge these wristbands once the funds are depleted, depending on the school calendar. To ease a parents mind as well, these wrist watches have been equiped with real time offline GPS cordinates that can be pinned to display the child's location.

The process at this time is quite grainy, however we have 2 approaches on the matter:

What they need to do is to flag down one of our select matatu partners, get on board and be transported to the nearest bus stop near their school with a base fare per day. This means that the commute wrristbands can only be charged twice a day, for the to and from commute.

The other alternative is to partner with regional churches that have buses/vans. Each van/bus will have a permanent transit route that covers 4 - 5 schools in the region. These buses/vans will run at limited times 6am - 10am, and 3pm - 5pm. What the students need to do in this case is wait for the bus/van at their assigned times. To reduce the confusion all wristbands will be color coded, coresponding to the bus/van route. So before a child boards a van the drivers have to ensure that the wrist bands corespond with the route color code.
